# Why and Goals

**Status:** Approved  
**Type:** Context and Goal Document  
**Audience:** Founder, Product, Design, Engineering, GTM  
**Last Updated:** 2026-04-05

---

## 1. Why this product must exist

Real organizations do not keep meaning in one place.

- strategy and definitions live in documents
- nuance and exceptions live in conversations
- execution state lives in issue trackers and operational systems
- policy lives in separate artifacts
- important decisions are spread across meetings, threads, comments, and memory

The problem is not only that information is scattered.  
The deeper problem is that **organizational context becomes fragmented and hard to reconstruct**.

---

## 2. The biggest pain Cornerstone addresses

The central pain is:

> **Documents, conversations, and systems are scattered, so AI does not know the organization’s actual context. For the same reason, humans also lose organizational context.**

That means humans and AI often cannot consistently reconstruct:
- what the organization already knew
- why a past decision was made
- what constraints shaped that decision
- what changed as a result
- what is official versus what is outdated, local, or draft

This is the root failure mode behind inaccurate answers, weak automation, and repeated context loss.

---

## 3. Failure mode of existing approaches

### 3.1 AI failure
AI can produce plausible language, but it often fails in organizations because it does not know:

- the exact meaning of company-specific terms
- which artifacts are official
- what prior decisions and constraints shaped the current state
- how one team’s terms differ from another’s
- what the actual decision lineage looks like

As a result, it can guide decisions in directions that sound reasonable but are organizationally wrong.

### 3.2 Human failure
This is not just an AI problem.

Humans also fail when:
- onboarding takes too long because the context is scattered
- prior decisions become hard to explain
- team language drifts over time
- policy and practice diverge
- institutional memory remains trapped in individuals or stale documents

So the true problem is not “AI lacks context.”  
The true problem is **the organization lacks a stable, shared, reconstructable context layer**.

---

## 4. The questions Cornerstone must answer well

Cornerstone should make the following questions reliably answerable.

- What does this term mean in this organization?
- How is this concept connected to systems, workflows, policies, or roles?
- Why was this decision made?
- What constraints and evidence shaped that decision?
- Is this term, relation, or decision official, draft, deprecated, or superseded?
- Where did this answer come from?
- Is the AI reasoning from the organization’s real context or from isolated fragments?

---

## 5. What changes if Cornerstone succeeds

### Before
- context is fragmented
- terms drift by team and document
- past decisions are hard to reconstruct
- humans and AI both operate from incomplete fragments
- trust in answers is weak because provenance is unclear

### After
- official concepts and relationships are curated
- decision background and impact are traceable
- humans and AI use the same context layer
- answers can point to evidence and source lineage
- the organization can reconstruct context, not just search for text

---

## 6. User outcomes

### For knowledge operators and domain owners
- easier source connection and sync monitoring
- clearer review and approval flow for official meaning
- less drift between teams and artifacts
- traceable rationale for important changes

### For general organization members
- faster understanding of official terms and relationships
- less reliance on individual memory
- better onboarding and collaboration

### For AI systems and agent builders
- access to official concepts, relations, and decision context
- better grounded answers and automation
- lower risk of plausible but wrong reasoning

---

## 7. North Star

Cornerstone’s North Star is:

> **For important organizational questions, humans and AI should be able to reconstruct the organization’s actual context, definitions, evidence, and decision background rather than relying on isolated fragments.**

A more operational version is:

> **Turn scattered artifacts into shared understanding.**

---

## 8. Success criteria

Cornerstone is succeeding when the following become true.

1. Important organizational questions can be answered with linked concept, relation, decision, and evidence structure.
2. Humans and AI can tell what is official, draft, deprecated, or superseded.
3. Past decisions become easier to explain and revisit.
4. Context loss between tools, teams, and time is meaningfully reduced.
5. AI output quality improves because it reasons from organizational memory rather than only from retrieved text.

---

## 9. Anti-goals

Cornerstone is not trying to become:

- a chat-only product
- a generic search engine
- a document warehouse
- a no-review auto-generated knowledge graph
- a single-model AI shell

The product must remain centered on **shared organizational context**.
