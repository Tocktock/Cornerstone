Review Suggested Evidence Map

What is an evidence map?

An evidence map is a reviewable summary of what CornerStone noticed in a document. It lists the important entities, their details, and their relationships so later claims and actions can reuse context without treating suggestions as truth.

  1. Entities: the things found in evidence, such as an organization, policy, person, event, asset, claim, or action.
  2. Details: facts about an entity, such as risk level or review date.
  3. Relationships: how entities connect, such as an organization governed by a policy.
Local VS1 proof only. This page does not claim production readiness, live connector readiness, or human acceptance.

What happened?

  1. Artifact waits to be ingested.
  2. Search waits for evidence snapshot.
  3. Suggested map waits for extracted entities, details, and relationships.
  4. Review waits for selected, rejected, and deferred suggestions.
  5. Approval waits for a versioned Map Update.
  6. Entity Profile waits for relationships, related claim/action, version, and audit evidence.
  7. Safety boundary waits for evidence-required claims and zero real external calls.

Why are these connected?

Review each relationship before promotion. Every row shows the edge, weight, plain-language description, and evidence line that suggested the connection.
